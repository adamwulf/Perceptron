## Description

Perceptron is a simple, hands-on demo of Frank Rosenblatt's 1958 perceptron — the first machine that could learn to recognize patterns.

Flip the toggle switches to set an input, turn the weight dials, and watch the analog meter and output LED respond in real time. It's a tactile, no-black-box way to get a feel for how a perceptron turns inputs and weights into an output.

Styled with a vintage 1950s control-panel look — clicking switches, turning knobs, and a swinging meter.

Great for students, educators, and anyone curious about the roots of neural networks.

FEATURES
• Vintage control panel — switches, rotary weight dials, analog meter, and LED output
• Turn dials to "train" the perceptron and watch the output change
• Simple, hands-on intro to how a perceptron works
• Designed for iPad and Mac
